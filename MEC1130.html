Key terms

Mechanism

Parts working together to transfer or control motion/energy.

In the shop: Identifying mechanisms inside a jack or clamp.

Mechanical advantage

Getting more output force than input force (trade-off with distance).

In the shop: Using a long handle to loosen a fastener.

Lever

Rigid bar that pivots to multiply force.

In the shop: Using a breaker bar for stubborn bolts.

Fulcrum

The pivot point of a lever.

In the shop: Placing a pry bar to lift a component.

Torque

A twisting force (force × distance).

In the shop: Torqueing lug nuts to spec.

Friction

Resistance between surfaces that slide/roll against each other.

In the shop: Explaining brake pads stopping a rotor.

Bearing

Part that reduces friction between moving components.

In the shop: Recognising a worn bearing by noise/roughness.

Gear

Toothed wheel that transfers motion and can change speed/torque.

In the shop: Comparing small vs large gear effects.

Gear ratio

Relationship between input and output speeds/torque.

In the shop: Explaining why low gear increases torque.

Belt drive

Flexible belt transferring motion between pulleys.

In the shop: Belt-driven accessories on an engine.

Pulley

Wheel that a belt runs on; changes direction/ratio.

In the shop: Idler pulley guiding a serpentine belt.

Chain drive

Chain and sprockets transferring motion without slip.

In the shop: Motorcycle chain drive basics.

Sprocket

Toothed wheel used with a chain.

In the shop: Inspecting sprocket wear patterns.

Cam

Converts rotary motion to up-and-down motion.

In the shop: Cam opening and closing valves.

Linkage

Connected rods/levers that transmit motion.

In the shop: Steering linkage moving wheels left/right.

Flywheel

Heavy rotating disc that stores energy and smooths rotation.

In the shop: Smoothing engine pulses or supporting a clutch.

Guarding

Covers/barriers that prevent contact with moving parts.

In the shop: Using a guard on rotating equipment.
