Key terms

Power assist

System that reduces driver effort using hydraulic or electric help.

In the shop: Power steering assist during parking.

Hydraulic power steering

Steering assist using a pump, fluid, and control valve.

In the shop: Checking fluid level and leaks at hoses.

Electric power steering (EPS)

Steering assist using an electric motor and module.

In the shop: Scanning for EPS codes and calibrations.

Power steering pump

Hydraulic pump that creates flow and pressure.

In the shop: Diagnosing whining noise from low fluid or pump wear.

Rack-and-pinion

Steering gear that converts steering wheel rotation to wheel movement.

In the shop: Inspecting rack boots for leaks.

Steering gear box

Steering box used on some trucks for mechanical advantage.

In the shop: Adjusting gear lash (if permitted) and checking wear.

Pressure line

High-pressure hose from pump to steering gear.

In the shop: Inspecting for seepage and cracked fittings.

Return line

Low-pressure hose returning fluid to reservoir.

In the shop: Checking hose clamps for leaks.

Fluid aeration

Air mixed into fluid causing noise and poor assist.

In the shop: Bleeding air after hose replacement.

Bleeding procedure

Steps to remove air from hydraulic system.

In the shop: Turning lock-to-lock per procedure with wheels off ground.

Assist curve

How much assist the system provides at different speeds/loads.

In the shop: Explaining why assist changes at highway speed.

Torque sensor

EPS sensor that measures driver steering input.

In the shop: Diagnosing pull or odd assist from sensor faults.

Calibration

A relearn/setup procedure for electronic systems.

In the shop: Performing steering angle or EPS calibration.

Safety support

Using proper stands and procedures when working on steering.

In the shop: Never working under a vehicle supported only by a jack.

Road test verification

Confirming steering feel, return-to-centre, and no leaks.

In the shop: Testing after repair and checking for codes.
