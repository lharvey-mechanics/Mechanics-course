Key terms

Metal finishing

Final shaping of metal so minimal filler is needed.

In the shop: Using hammer and dolly to smooth a repaired area.

Pulling

Using tools to pull dents outward.

In the shop: Using a stud welder and slide hammer on a dent.

Stud welder

Tool that welds small studs to pull damaged panels.

In the shop: Pulling a quarter panel dent without backside access.

Slide hammer

Tool used to pull on studs or hooks.

In the shop: Pulling a crease in controlled steps.

Hammer and dolly

Tools used together to move and smooth metal.

In the shop: Planishing a dent to remove ripples.

Heat shrinking

Using heat to reduce stretched metal (when appropriate).

In the shop: Shrinking an oil-can area carefully.

Panel beating

Shaping metal using controlled strikes and support.

In the shop: Restoring a body line after a hit.

Body filler

Filler used to level small imperfections after metal work.

In the shop: Applying thin filler after proper metal repair.

Featheredge

Tapering paint edges so repairs blend.

In the shop: Sanding around a repair before primer.

Primer surfacer

Primer used to fill sanding scratches and minor lows.

In the shop: Spraying surfacer before block sanding.

Block sanding

Using a block to keep surfaces flat.

In the shop: Blocking primer to remove waves.

Panel alignment

Setting correct gaps and flushness of panels.

In the shop: Adjusting door hinges to set even gaps.

Fastener torque

Tightening fasteners to specification.

In the shop: Torqueing hinge bolts to spec.

Safety PPE

Personal protective equipment for repair work.

In the shop: Eye protection and gloves during grinding and welding.

Clean-up and corrosion protection

Steps to protect bare metal and clean workspace.

In the shop: Applying weld-through primer and seam sealer.
