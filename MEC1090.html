Key terms

Electricity

The movement of electrical charge that can power components.

In the shop: Explaining how power reaches a headlight bulb.

Voltage (V)

Electrical “push” that drives current through a circuit.

In the shop: Measuring battery voltage with a multimeter.

Current (A)

The flow rate of electricity through a circuit.

In the shop: Checking parasitic draw in amps.

Resistance (Ω)

How much a material opposes current flow.

In the shop: Measuring a resistor or checking a bad connection.

Ohm’s Law

Relationship between voltage, current, and resistance (V = I × R).

In the shop: Predicting how current changes if resistance increases.

Power (W)

How fast electrical energy is used (work per second).

In the shop: Comparing wattage of bulbs or accessories.

Series circuit

One path for current; if one part opens, everything stops.

In the shop: Old-style string lights: one bulb fails, all go out.

Parallel circuit

Multiple paths; one failure doesn’t stop the others.

In the shop: Left and right headlights on separate branches.

Ground

The return path to the battery (often the vehicle body).

In the shop: Finding a weak ground causing dim lights.

Continuity

Whether a path is unbroken so current can flow.

In the shop: Continuity test on a fuse or wire.

Open circuit

A break in the path—current cannot flow.

In the shop: Broken wire causing a component not to work.

Short circuit

Unwanted path (often to ground) that can cause high current.

In the shop: Pinched wire blowing a fuse.

Fuse

A safety device that melts to protect wiring from overcurrent.

In the shop: Replacing a blown fuse after diagnosing the cause.

Relay

An electrically controlled switch (low current controls high current).

In the shop: Using a relay to power a cooling fan circuit.

Multimeter

Tool for measuring voltage, resistance, and current.

In the shop: Checking charging system voltage at idle.

Battery electrolyte

The liquid in a lead-acid battery that supports chemical reaction.

In the shop: Discussing safety for spills and acid exposure.

Specific gravity

A hydrometer reading showing battery charge state.

In the shop: Using a hydrometer to test battery health.

Electrical hazard

Risk of shock, burns, fire, or explosion from electrical work.

In the shop: Explaining battery explosion causes and prevention.
