Key terms

Refinish process

Steps from prep to basecoat and clearcoat.

In the shop: Planning sanding, masking, priming, paint, polish.

Feather edge

Tapered transition from old finish to repaired area.

In the shop: Preparing a blend area around a repair.

Primer surfacer

High-build primer that fills scratches and minor lows.

In the shop: Spraying surfacer then block sanding.

Sealer

Coating that creates a uniform surface before colour.

In the shop: Applying sealer to improve colour consistency.

Basecoat

Colour layer of a paint system.

In the shop: Applying basecoat to matched colour.

Clearcoat

Protective top layer that provides gloss and UV protection.

In the shop: Applying clear for durability and shine.

Blend panel

Adjacent panel sprayed to hide colour differences.

In the shop: Blending into a door when painting a fender.

Colour match

Matching paint colour to the vehicle.

In the shop: Using spray-out cards to confirm colour.

Spray-out card

Test panel sprayed to evaluate colour and flop.

In the shop: Comparing spray-out to vehicle under different light.

Gun setup

Adjusting spray gun (pressure, fan, fluid) for proper pattern.

In the shop: Setting fan pattern to avoid runs or dry spray.

Flash time

Wait time between coats for solvents to evaporate.

In the shop: Allowing proper flash before next coat.

Orange peel

Bumpy texture in paint finish.

In the shop: Adjusting gun setup and technique to reduce texture.

Run/sag

Paint that flows downward due to too much material.

In the shop: Correcting technique and later denibbing/polishing.

Denib

Removing dust nibs or small defects from clearcoat.

In the shop: Sanding a nib with fine grit before polishing.

Polishing

Restoring gloss after sanding defects.

In the shop: Using compound and polish to restore shine.
