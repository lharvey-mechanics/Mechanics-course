Key terms

Damage analysis

Systematic inspection to identify all damage and required repairs.

In the shop: Assessing visible and hidden damage after a collision.

Impact direction

Where the force came from; helps predict hidden damage.

In the shop: Side impact likely affects door, B-pillar, rocker.

Primary damage

The most obvious damage at the impact area.

In the shop: Crushed bumper cover.

Secondary damage

Damage caused by movement/force transfer.

In the shop: Radiator support pushed back from bumper hit.

Hidden damage

Damage not seen until parts are removed.

In the shop: Bent absorber behind bumper cover.

Structural damage

Damage to parts that affect strength and crash performance.

In the shop: Bent frame rail or unibody rail.

Non-structural damage

Damage mostly cosmetic or replaceable bolt-on parts.

In the shop: Scratched trim or dented fender skin.

Corrosion protection

Steps to prevent rust after repairs.

In the shop: Reapplying seam sealer and cavity wax.

Crush zone

Area designed to deform to absorb energy.

In the shop: Front rails designed to crumple in a crash.

Repair plan

Order of operations to complete repair safely and correctly.

In the shop: Deciding when to measure, pull, weld, and refinish.

Measuring system

Tools used to measure body dimensions (tram, datum, electronic).

In the shop: Checking diagonals to confirm square.

Sectioning

Cutting and replacing part of a panel/rail per OEM rules.

In the shop: Sectioning a quarter panel at approved locations.

Weld type

MIG, spot, plug, or other weld method required.

In the shop: Choosing plug welds where spot welds were removed.

OEM procedures

Manufacturer-approved repair methods and restrictions.

In the shop: Following OEM warnings about heat on UHSS steel.

Pre-fit

Test fitting panels before final fastening/paint.

In the shop: Hanging a door to check gaps before painting.
