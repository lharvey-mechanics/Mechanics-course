Key terms

Wiring diagram

A map showing circuits, connections, and component locations.

In the shop: Using a diagram to find splice points.

Circuit tracing

Following a circuit step-by-step to locate a fault.

In the shop: Checking power at fuse, then switch, then load.

Continuity test

Checking for an unbroken path in a wire or component.

In the shop: Testing a wire from connector pin to pin.

Short to ground

Wire contacts ground when it should not.

In the shop: Blowing a fuse immediately when a circuit is powered.

Short to power

Wire contacts a powered source incorrectly.

In the shop: A component stays on even when switched off.

Open circuit

A break in the circuit that stops current flow.

In the shop: Broken wire inside insulation causing intermittent open.

Back probing

Testing a connector from the rear without damaging it.

In the shop: Measuring signal voltage at a sensor connector.

Terminal tension

How tightly a connector terminal grips the mating pin.

In the shop: Loose terminal causing intermittent electrical issue.

Crimping

Joining wires using a crimp connector and tool.

In the shop: Using butt splices with heat-shrink for harness repair.

Heat-shrink

Tubing that shrinks when heated to seal/insulate repairs.

In the shop: Sealing a crimped connection to prevent corrosion.

Wire gauge

Wire thickness; affects current capacity.

In the shop: Selecting correct gauge wire for an accessory circuit.

Strain relief

Preventing a repair from being pulled/bent repeatedly.

In the shop: Securing harness so movement doesn’t stress the splice.
