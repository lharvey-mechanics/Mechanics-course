Key terms

Panel damage

Dents/creases/stretching that change panel shape.

In the shop: Assessing a door dent before repair.

High spot / low spot

Raised or sunken area on a panel.

In the shop: Using a straightedge to find low spots.

Stretching

Metal becomes larger/thinner from impact.

In the shop: Large dent that “oil cans.”

Shrinking

Reducing stretched metal back toward original shape.

In the shop: Heat shrinking a stretched area carefully.

Body hammer

Tool used to shape metal during repair.

In the shop: Tapping out a dent with controlled strikes.

Dolly

Hand-held anvil supporting metal while hammering.

In the shop: Hammer-and-dolly work to restore shape.

Hammer-on-dolly

Hammering with dolly directly behind the hit area.

In the shop: Smoothing a dent by planishing.

Hammer-off-dolly

Hammering near the dolly to move metal.

In the shop: Controlling metal movement without stretching too much.

Planishing

Light hammering to smooth metal surface.

In the shop: Final smoothing before minimal filler.

Pick hammer

Pointed hammer for precise metal movement.

In the shop: Lifting a low spot carefully.

Stud welder

Tool that welds studs to pull dents.

In the shop: Pulling a dent without backside access.

Slide hammer

Pulling tool used with studs or attachments.

In the shop: Pulling a crease outward in stages.

Panel alignment

Ensuring gaps and fit match factory lines.

In the shop: Checking door gaps after repair.

Featheredge

Tapered edge between old finish and repaired area.

In the shop: Sanding edges so repairs blend smoothly.

Body filler

Polyester filler used to level minor imperfections.

In the shop: Applying thin filler after metal work is close.

Sanding grit

Abrasive size; lower number = coarser.

In the shop: Choosing 80/180/320 grit for stages.

Heat hazard

Risks from torch/heat tools (fire, burns).

In the shop: Protecting interiors and PPE during heat shrinking.
