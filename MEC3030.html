Key terms

Symptom

What the engine is doing that is not normal.

In the shop: “Rough idle” is a symptom.

Root cause

The real reason the problem is happening.

In the shop: Vacuum leak causing lean misfire.

Diagnostic strategy

A logical plan to test possibilities instead of guessing.

In the shop: Starting with basics: air, fuel, spark, compression.

Scan tool

Device used to read codes and live data.

In the shop: Checking misfire counters and fuel trims.

DTC

Diagnostic Trouble Code stored when a fault is detected.

In the shop: Interpreting P0301 as cylinder 1 misfire.

Fuel trim

PCM adjustments to fuel delivery (short-term and long-term).

In the shop: High positive trims suggesting a lean condition.

Vacuum leak

Unmetered air entering the engine.

In the shop: Smoke testing intake for leaks.

Compression test

Measures cylinder pressure during cranking.

In the shop: Low compression indicating mechanical issue.

Cylinder leak-down test

Adds air to a cylinder to find where it leaks.

In the shop: Air heard in intake suggests intake valve leak.

Ignition secondary

High-voltage side of ignition system (coils, wires, plugs).

In the shop: Checking spark plug condition and gap.

Fuel pressure

Fuel system pressure required for proper operation.

In the shop: Testing pressure vs spec under load.

Injector balance test

Checks if injectors deliver similar fuel amounts.

In the shop: Finding a restricted injector on one cylinder.

Sensor correlation

Comparing sensor signals to see if they agree logically.

In the shop: Comparing MAP and TPS at idle.

Mode $06

OBD test results for monitors (where supported).

In the shop: Using Mode $06 to evaluate misfire or catalyst data.

Verification

Confirming the repair fixed the symptom and no codes return.

In the shop: Road testing and rechecking live data.
