Key terms

Surface contamination

Anything that prevents paint sticking (oil, wax, silicone).

In the shop: Fish-eyes caused by silicone contamination.

Adhesion

How well coatings stick to a surface.

In the shop: Poor adhesion causes peeling or flaking.

Wax and grease remover

Cleaner used before sanding/painting.

In the shop: Wiping panels before prep work.

Masking

Covering areas to protect from overspray/dust.

In the shop: Masking trim and glass before sanding.

Sanding

Abrading a surface to smooth and create “tooth.”

In the shop: Scuffing clear coat for recoat.

Mechanical tooth

Scratch pattern that helps coatings bond.

In the shop: Proper scuff for primer/paint to grip.

Dry sanding

Sanding without water, often with dust control.

In the shop: Using a DA sander with vacuum.

Wet sanding

Sanding with water to reduce clogging and refine finish.

In the shop: Wet sanding primer before base coat.

DA sander

Dual-action sander for efficient, even sanding.

In the shop: Flattening primer surfacer evenly.

Feather edging

Tapering edges so repair blends smoothly.

In the shop: Smoothing paint edge around a chip.

Rust removal

Removing corrosion before refinishing.

In the shop: Wire wheel or sanding to clean metal.

Primer

Undercoat that improves adhesion and fills minor scratches.

In the shop: Applying primer after bare metal exposure.

Primer surfacer

Thicker primer to fill sanding scratches/low texture.

In the shop: Building surface before final sand.

Guide coat

Light coating used to reveal highs/lows during sanding.

In the shop: Spotting low areas while block sanding.

Block sanding

Using a sanding block to keep panels flat.

In the shop: Preventing waves on a large panel.

Paint defect

Imperfection such as peeling, cracking, fisheyes, or orange peel.

In the shop: Identifying defect type before repair.

Abrasive dust hazard

Dust can harm lungs/eyes and contaminate paint.

In the shop: Using proper PPE and dust control.

VOC/chemical hazard

Fumes that can be harmful from solvents/coatings.

In the shop: Using ventilation and correct respirator.
