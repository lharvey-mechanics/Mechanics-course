Key terms

Appraisal

A structured estimate of a vehicle’s value using evidence and standards.

In the shop: Completing an appraisal form with condition notes.

Market comparison

Comparing similar vehicles to estimate value.

In the shop: Using local listings for year/make/model/trim.

Condition rating

A consistent way to describe the vehicle’s wear and appearance.

In the shop: Rating paint, interior, and mechanical condition.

Depreciation

Value loss from age, mileage, and condition.

In the shop: Adjusting value for high mileage.

Salvage title

A vehicle branded after being written off or heavily damaged.

In the shop: Reducing value due to a salvage status.

Write-off categories

Insurance classifications such as salvage, rebuilt, or non-repairable.

In the shop: Explaining why non-repairable cannot return to road.

Labour rate

Cost per hour used to estimate repairs.

In the shop: Applying a shop rate to a repair estimate.

Flat-rate time

Standard labour time for a job from a guide.

In the shop: Using flat-rate time to price a repair.

Parts estimate

Expected cost of parts needed for a repair.

In the shop: Pricing a bumper cover and clips.

Refinish time

Time required to prepare and paint a panel.

In the shop: Estimating time to refinish a fender.

Blend

Painting into an adjacent panel to match colour.

In the shop: Adding blend time on an estimate.

Supplement

An added cost approved after hidden damage is found.

In the shop: Writing a supplement after teardown reveals more damage.

Deductible

Amount the customer pays before insurance covers the rest.

In the shop: Explaining how deductible affects the final bill.

OEM vs aftermarket

Original parts vs non-original replacement parts.

In the shop: Comparing fit/cost differences in an estimate.

Documentation

Clear notes, photos, and measurements that support an appraisal.

In the shop: Adding photos of damage and VIN labels.
