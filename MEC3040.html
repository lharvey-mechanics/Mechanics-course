Key terms

Tune-up

Service work that restores engine performance and reliability.

In the shop: Replacing plugs and inspecting ignition components.

Maintenance schedule

Manufacturer recommended service timing and tasks.

In the shop: Using a schedule to plan tune-up items.

Spark plug

Component that ignites air–fuel mixture in gasoline engines.

In the shop: Inspecting plug wear and correct gap.

Plug gap

Distance between spark plug electrodes.

In the shop: Adjusting gap to specification (when applicable).

Ignition coil

Device that increases voltage for the spark.

In the shop: Testing coil output or swapping for diagnosis.

Air filter

Filter that removes dirt from intake air.

In the shop: Replacing a clogged air filter to improve airflow.

Fuel filter

Filter that removes contaminants from fuel (if serviceable).

In the shop: Checking serviceability and replacement interval.

PCV valve

Crankcase ventilation valve that controls blow-by gases.

In the shop: Replacing a stuck PCV causing oil leaks or idle issues.

Throttle body service

Cleaning carbon from throttle plate and bore (when appropriate).

In the shop: Cleaning throttle body to address sticky throttle/idle.

MAF sensor

Measures incoming air mass for fuel calculation.

In the shop: Using proper cleaner to avoid sensor damage.

Battery service

Checking battery condition and terminals.

In the shop: Cleaning corrosion and testing state of health.

Belt inspection

Checking belts for cracks, glazing, and tension.

In the shop: Replacing a cracked serpentine belt.

Cooling system check

Inspecting coolant level, condition, and leaks.

In the shop: Testing coolant concentration and cap pressure.

Emissions check

Confirming basic emissions-related systems are functioning.

In the shop: Checking for codes and monitor readiness.

Final quality check

Confirming no leaks, no loose fasteners, and good running condition.

In the shop: Road test and re-check after service.
