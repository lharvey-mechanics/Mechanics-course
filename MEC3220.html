Key terms

Auto glass

Vehicle glass including windshield, side, and back glass.

In the shop: Identifying laminated vs tempered glass.

Laminated glass

Two layers with a plastic interlayer; usually windshields.

In the shop: Windshield cracks but stays together.

Tempered glass

Heat-treated glass that shatters into small pieces.

In the shop: Side glass breaking into pellets.

Urethane adhesive

Primary adhesive used to bond windshields.

In the shop: Applying correct bead size and cure time.

Pinchweld

The body flange where the windshield bonds.

In the shop: Cleaning and priming pinchweld before install.

Primer

Chemical that promotes adhesion and protects against corrosion/UV.

In the shop: Priming glass and pinchweld per adhesive system.

Safe drive-away time

Minimum time before vehicle can be driven safely after install.

In the shop: Waiting required time before releasing vehicle.

Moulding/trim

Exterior pieces around glass that may need removal/replacement.

In the shop: Replacing damaged moulding clips.

Glass removal tool

Tools used to cut urethane and remove glass.

In the shop: Using a cold knife or wire system safely.

Glass setting blocks

Supports that position glass correctly in the opening.

In the shop: Ensuring windshield sits at proper height.

ADAS calibration

Recalibrating cameras/sensors after windshield replacement (when required).

In the shop: Calibrating lane-keep camera after install.

Leak test

Checking for water/air leaks after installation.

In the shop: Spraying water and inspecting for leaks.

Safety PPE

Eye and cut protection for glass work.

In the shop: Cut-resistant gloves and safety glasses.

Disposal

Proper handling and disposal of broken glass.

In the shop: Using designated bin and safe cleanup procedures.

Verification road test

Confirming no wind noise and sensors function.

In the shop: Test drive and confirm ADAS warning lights are off.
