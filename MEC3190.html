Key terms

Sectioning procedure

Replacing a portion of a panel/structure per OEM rules.

In the shop: Sectioning a quarter panel at approved joints.

Plug weld

Weld made in a hole to replace a spot weld location.

In the shop: Plug welding along a flange after panel replacement.

MIG welding

Common welding method used for body repairs.

In the shop: Setting machine parameters for sheet metal thickness.

Weld-through primer

Primer used where panels overlap to reduce corrosion.

In the shop: Applying to flanges before welding.

Panel replacement

Removing a damaged panel and installing a new one.

In the shop: Replacing a door shell or outer skin.

Structural straightening

Pulling/straightening structural parts using approved methods.

In the shop: Using a frame rack to pull a rail to spec.

Measuring and verification

Confirming dimensions during and after repairs.

In the shop: Re-checking diagonals after each pull.

Adhesive bonding

Using structural adhesives as specified by OEM.

In the shop: Bonding a roof skin using approved adhesive.

Rivet bonding

Using rivets with adhesive, common on aluminium structures.

In the shop: Replacing rivets per pattern and tool requirements.

Heat-affected zone

Area of metal changed by heat; can weaken high-strength steels.

In the shop: Avoiding excessive heat on UHSS parts.

UHSS

Ultra-high-strength steel used in modern vehicles.

In the shop: Following OEM rules: replace, do not straighten.

Corrosion protection steps

Coatings, seam sealer, cavity wax after repair.

In the shop: Applying cavity wax inside repaired rocker panel.

Fit and finish

Ensuring panels align and close correctly.

In the shop: Checking door closes smoothly and gaps are even.

Quality control inspection

Final safety and workmanship checks.

In the shop: Checking weld quality, fasteners, and rustproofing.

Documentation

Photos and notes to support repair decisions.

In the shop: Recording section locations and weld counts.
