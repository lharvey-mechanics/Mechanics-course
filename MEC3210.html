Key terms

Plastic identification

Determining plastic type for correct repair method.

In the shop: Reading material codes on bumper cover (PP, TPO).

Thermoplastic

Plastic that can be reshaped with heat.

In the shop: Heat reshaping a bumper cover carefully.

Thermoset

Plastic/composite that does not remelt once cured.

In the shop: Fibreglass panel repair using resin.

Plastic welding

Joining plastic using heat and filler rod.

In the shop: Welding a crack on a bumper cover.

Nitrogen plastic welder

Plastic welder that uses nitrogen to reduce oxidation.

In the shop: Repairing TPO bumper with nitrogen welder.

Plastic adhesive repair

Using adhesives to repair cracks or breaks.

In the shop: Bonding tabs with two-part plastic adhesive.

V-groove

A groove cut into a crack to allow strong repair fill.

In the shop: Grinding a V-groove before welding or adhesive.

Reinforcement mesh

Material embedded to strengthen a plastic repair.

In the shop: Adding mesh on backside of a repaired crack.

Fibreglass mat

Woven or chopped fibres used with resin for repairs.

In the shop: Laying mat and resin to repair a fibreglass panel.

Resin and hardener

Materials that cure to create a strong composite.

In the shop: Mixing correct ratio for proper cure.

Surface prep

Cleaning, sanding, and keying surfaces for bonding.

In the shop: Using plastic cleaner and proper grit before repair.

Flex additive

Additive that helps coatings flex on plastic parts (system dependent).

In the shop: Using recommended additive for flexible bumper parts.

Featheredge

Tapering edges so repair blends smoothly.

In the shop: Sanding surrounding area before primer.

Primer for plastics

Primer designed to adhere to plastic substrates.

In the shop: Applying adhesion promoter before primer.

Safety (dust/fumes)

Risks from sanding plastics/fibreglass and using resins.

In the shop: Using respirator and ventilation during sanding.
