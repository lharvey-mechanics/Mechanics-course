Key terms

Starting system

The components that crank the engine (battery, starter, control circuit).

In the shop: Tracing the start signal from ignition switch to starter.

Starter motor

Electric motor that turns the engine to start it.

In the shop: Bench testing a starter for engagement and speed.

Solenoid

Electromagnetic switch that engages the starter and closes high-current contacts.

In the shop: Checking solenoid click but no crank condition.

Starter relay

Low-current relay controlling starter solenoid power.

In the shop: Swapping relay with a known-good one for testing.

Control circuit

The low-current circuit that commands the starter.

In the shop: Testing for start signal at the relay coil.

High-current circuit

The heavy wiring carrying starter current.

In the shop: Checking battery cable condition and tightness.

Voltage drop (cranking)

Measured voltage loss during starting load.

In the shop: Finding a corroded positive cable causing slow crank.

Current draw

How many amps the starter uses while cranking.

In the shop: Using an amp clamp to compare draw to spec.

No-crank

Starter does not rotate the engine.

In the shop: Diagnosing a no-crank with relay and signal checks.

Slow crank

Starter turns engine too slowly to start.

In the shop: Identifying weak battery or high resistance in cables.

Neutral safety switch

Prevents starting unless in Park/Neutral (or clutch depressed).

In the shop: Testing switch when vehicle starts only sometimes.

Pinion engagement

Starter gear meshes with flywheel/flexplate.

In the shop: Grinding noise due to worn teeth or misalignment.
