Key terms

Preventive maintenance

Regular service done to reduce breakdowns and extend vehicle life.

In the shop: Following a scheduled oil change interval and inspection list.

Service interval

The recommended time or mileage between maintenance services.

In the shop: Checking the owner’s manual for oil change intervals.

Inspection checklist

A step-by-step list used to ensure nothing is missed.

In the shop: Completing a walk-around and under-hood inspection form.

Fluid condition

The colour, smell, and feel of a fluid that indicates health or problems.

In the shop: Noting burnt-smelling ATF as a warning sign.

Wear pattern

Clues on a part that show how it has been used or misused.

In the shop: Uneven tire wear suggesting alignment issues.

Torque specification

The correct tightness value for a fastener.

In the shop: Torqueing wheel nuts to the vehicle spec.

Service information

Trusted procedures/specs from manuals, databases, or OEM sources.

In the shop: Using AllData/Mitchell/OEM steps for a brake service.

Work order

A written record of the job, steps, and customer/teacher notes.

In the shop: Writing the concern, cause, and correction after inspection.

Quality check

Final verification that work is correct and safe.

In the shop: Confirming no leaks and brake pedal feel is normal.

Road test

A controlled drive to confirm a repair or diagnose a concern.

In the shop: Listening for noise changes after wheel bearing service.

Shop supplies

Consumables used during work (rags, cleaners, sealants).

In the shop: Tracking brake cleaner and shop towel use appropriately.

Environmental handling

Proper methods for storing and disposing of fluids and waste.

In the shop: Using a waste-oil tank and approved absorbent for spills.
