Key terms

Estimate

A written plan of labour, parts, and operations to repair damage.

In the shop: Creating a repair estimate for a quarter panel hit.

Labour operation

A specific task on an estimate with time and rate.

In the shop: Adding time for remove/install a bumper cover.

Labour rate

Cost per hour used to calculate labour charges.

In the shop: Applying shop rate to body labour and refinish labour.

Refinish labour

Work related to paint preparation and spraying.

In the shop: Estimating prime, block, basecoat, clearcoat time.

Blend operation

Painting into adjacent panels to match colour.

In the shop: Adding blend time for door when fender is painted.

R&I (remove and install)

Removing a part and reinstalling it after repair.

In the shop: R&I headlamp assembly for access.

R&R (remove and replace)

Removing a part and replacing it with a new one.

In the shop: Replacing a damaged bumper absorber.

Sublet

Work sent outside the shop (e.g., alignment, glass).

In the shop: Adding sublet for wheel alignment after suspension repair.

Supplement

Additional charges after hidden damage is discovered.

In the shop: Writing a supplement after teardown.

Parts type

OEM, aftermarket, recycled, or reconditioned parts.

In the shop: Choosing OEM vs aftermarket based on fit and policy.

Repair vs replace

Decision based on safety, cost, and manufacturer rules.

In the shop: Replacing a structural part instead of straightening it.

Documentation photos

Photos that support damage and required operations.

In the shop: Taking photos of VIN labels and damage areas.

Pre-repair scan

Scanning vehicle systems before repairs (where required).

In the shop: Recording codes and module status prior to repair.

Post-repair scan

Scanning after repair to confirm systems are normal.

In the shop: Clearing codes and verifying calibrations.

Quality control

Final checks for fit, finish, and safety.

In the shop: Checking panel gaps and ensuring no warning lights.
