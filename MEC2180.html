Key terms

Charging system

Components that keep the battery charged (alternator, regulator, wiring).

In the shop: Testing charging voltage with accessories on.

Alternator

Generator that produces electrical power when engine runs.

In the shop: Confirming alternator output at idle and higher rpm.

Voltage regulator

Controls alternator output voltage.

In the shop: Diagnosing overcharging due to a failed regulator.

Charge indicator

Warning lamp/message for charging faults.

In the shop: Battery light on dash indicating system problem.

Field circuit

Control circuit that energizes alternator to produce output.

In the shop: Checking field command with a scan tool (where applicable).

Diode

One-way electrical component; alternators use diodes in rectifier.

In the shop: Failed diode causing AC ripple and battery drain.

Rectifier

Converts alternator AC into DC for the vehicle.

In the shop: Checking ripple voltage to diagnose rectifier issues.

AC ripple

Unwanted AC voltage in a DC system, often from diode failure.

In the shop: Measuring ripple with a multimeter set to AC volts.

Charging voltage

System voltage while running (typically around 13.5–14.8 V).

In the shop: Comparing measured voltage to spec.

Charging current

Amps delivered by alternator to supply loads and charge battery.

In the shop: Using an amp clamp to see alternator output.

Parasitic draw

Key-off current draw that can drain a battery.

In the shop: Measuring draw after modules go to sleep.

Battery state of charge

How full the battery is, as a percentage.

In the shop: Using a tester to check state of charge before diagnosis.
