Key terms

Service information (OEM)

Manufacturer procedures and specifications for a specific vehicle.

In the shop: Finding correct torque for a caliper bracket bolt.

Diagnostic flow chart

Step-by-step troubleshooting path to isolate a fault.

In the shop: Following a no-start flow chart rather than guessing.

Symptom

What the vehicle is doing that is not normal.

In the shop: “Hard starting when cold” is a symptom.

Root cause

The real reason the problem is happening.

In the shop: A loose ground causing multiple electrical issues.

Test plan

A sequence of checks to confirm or rule out possible causes.

In the shop: Checking battery state, then voltage drop, then starter draw.

Baseline

A known-good reference measurement for comparison.

In the shop: Comparing sensor readings to spec or a similar working vehicle.

Specification

The acceptable measurement range or value.

In the shop: Brake rotor thickness minimum spec.

Voltage drop

Loss of voltage across a connection or wire under load.

In the shop: Testing a corroded ground strap during cranking.

Load test

Testing a component under working conditions.

In the shop: Battery load test to check capacity.

Intermittent fault

A problem that comes and goes.

In the shop: Loose connector causing random stalling.

Verification

Proving the fix worked and the symptom is gone.

In the shop: Repeat the original conditions and confirm no code returns.

Documentation

Recording steps, measurements, and results.

In the shop: Writing readings and decisions on the work order.
