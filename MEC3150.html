Key terms

Alignment

Adjusting wheel angles so the vehicle tracks straight and tires wear evenly.

In the shop: Performing a 4-wheel alignment on an alignment rack.

Toe

Angle where tires point inward or outward when viewed from above.

In the shop: Correcting toe to prevent rapid tire wear.

Camber

Inward/outward tilt of wheel when viewed from the front.

In the shop: Diagnosing inside-edge wear from negative camber.

Caster

Steering axis angle that affects stability and return-to-centre.

In the shop: Explaining why caster improves straight-line stability.

Thrust angle

Direction the rear wheels push the vehicle.

In the shop: Correcting rear alignment to prevent steering off-centre.

Steering angle sensor

Sensor used by stability/assist systems; may need calibration.

In the shop: Calibrating after alignment.

Ride height

Vehicle height that affects alignment geometry.

In the shop: Checking ride height before measuring angles.

Setback

Difference in wheelbase side-to-side; can indicate damage.

In the shop: Using measurement to suspect frame/suspension damage.

SAI (steering axis inclination)

Angle that helps diagnose bent parts.

In the shop: Comparing SAI readings to identify a bent knuckle.

Included angle

Camber + SAI; used for diagnosis.

In the shop: Using included angle to detect bent suspension.

Adjustment points

Hardware used to change angles (cams, eccentrics, shims).

In the shop: Adjusting cam bolts on a strut suspension.

Alignment specifications

Factory target and allowable ranges.

In the shop: Setting angles to OEM targets, not just “green.”

Tire wear diagnosis

Using wear patterns to identify alignment or suspension issues.

In the shop: Feathering on tires suggesting toe error.

Pre-alignment inspection

Checking parts before aligning (ball joints, tie rods, bearings).

In the shop: Rejecting alignment until worn tie rod is replaced.

Post-alignment road test

Confirming straight tracking and centred steering wheel.

In the shop: Test drive and verify steering wheel position.
